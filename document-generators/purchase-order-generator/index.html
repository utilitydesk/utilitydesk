What is a Purchase Order?

A Purchase Order (PO) is a legally binding commercial document issued by a buyer to a seller, indicating the types, quantities, and agreed prices for products or services. It serves as an official confirmation of a purchase and forms the basis of the transaction between the two parties.

In the Indian business context, purchase orders are essential for maintaining proper procurement records, ensuring tax compliance under GST, and establishing clear terms of trade. They protect both buyers and sellers by documenting the exact specifications of what is being purchased, at what price, and under what conditions.

Key Components of a Purchase Order

A professional purchase order in India should include the following essential elements:

  • PO Number and Date: Unique identification number and issue date for tracking and reference
  • Buyer Details: Company name, complete address, and GST identification number
  • Seller Details: Vendor name, address, and GST number for tax compliance
  • Item Specifications: Detailed description of products or services, including quantities and unit prices
  • Tax Details: Applicable GST rates (CGST, SGST, IGST) and tax amounts
  • Payment Terms: Due date, payment method, and any advance payment requirements
  • Delivery Terms: Expected delivery date, shipping address, and delivery instructions
  • Authorized Signatures: Signatures of authorized personnel from both parties

Purchase Order Format in India

The purchase order format in India has evolved to meet GST compliance requirements while maintaining simplicity and clarity. A standard Indian PO follows a structured format that includes all necessary information for legal validity and tax documentation.

Standard PO Structure

Indian purchase orders typically follow this structure:

  • Header Section: PO title, number, date, and buyer company logo (if any)
  • Party Information: Clear separation of buyer (bill from) and seller (bill to) details with GST numbers
  • Item Table: Organized table with item descriptions, HSN codes (for goods), quantities, unit prices, tax rates, and line totals
  • Tax Breakdown: Separate calculation of CGST and SGST for intra-state transactions, or IGST for inter-state transactions
  • Summary Section: Subtotal, tax amounts, grand total, and any additional charges or discounts
  • Terms and Conditions: Payment terms, delivery schedule, warranty information, and return policies
  • Authorization: Space for signatures, names, and designations of authorized signatories

GST Compliance in Purchase Orders

Under the GST regime, purchase orders serve as important documents for input tax credit claims. The PO must clearly mention:

  • GSTIN (GST Identification Number) of both buyer and seller
  • Place of supply to determine whether IGST or CGST+SGST applies
  • HSN code for goods (mandatory for businesses with turnover above ₹5 crore)
  • Separate breakup of CGST, SGST, and IGST amounts
  • Reverse charge applicability, if any

Purchase Order vs Invoice

While purchase orders and invoices are related documents in the procurement cycle, they serve different purposes and are issued at different stages of a transaction.

Key Differences

  • Purpose: A PO is a request to purchase goods/services, while an invoice is a request for payment after delivery
  • Issued By: PO is issued by the buyer, invoice is issued by the seller
  • Timing: PO comes before the transaction, invoice comes after goods/services are delivered
  • Legal Status: PO becomes binding upon acceptance; invoice is a demand for payment
  • Content Focus: PO focuses on what is being ordered; invoice focuses on what was delivered and payment due

The Procurement Cycle

The typical procurement cycle in Indian businesses follows this sequence:

  • Purchase Requisition: Internal request for goods/services
  • Purchase Order: Official order sent to vendor
  • Goods Receipt: Physical receipt and inspection of goods
  • Invoice Receipt: Vendor sends invoice for payment
  • Three-Way Matching: Matching PO, goods receipt, and invoice
  • Payment: Release payment as per agreed terms

Benefits of Purchase Orders

Using purchase orders provides numerous advantages for businesses of all sizes in India:

Legal Protection

Purchase orders create a legally binding contract between buyer and seller. They clearly document the terms of the transaction, protecting both parties in case of disputes. If a vendor delivers different quantities or quality than specified, the PO serves as evidence of the original agreement.

Budget Control

POs help businesses maintain control over spending by requiring approval before purchases are made. This prevents unauthorized spending and ensures all purchases align with budget allocations. Finance teams can track committed funds and forecast cash flow more accurately.

Inventory Management

Purchase orders help businesses plan inventory levels by documenting expected deliveries. This prevents stockouts and overstocking, optimizing working capital. Warehouse teams can prepare for incoming goods and allocate storage space efficiently.

Audit Trail

POs create a clear audit trail for all procurement activities. Auditors can trace every purchase from requisition to payment, ensuring compliance with internal controls and regulatory requirements. This is particularly important for GST audits and tax assessments.

Vendor Relationships

Professional purchase orders demonstrate organizational maturity and help build trust with vendors. Clear POs reduce misunderstandings and disputes, leading to smoother transactions and better vendor relationships. Vendors appreciate detailed POs as they reduce the need for clarifications.

Tax Compliance

Under GST, purchase orders help businesses claim input tax credit correctly. They provide documentation for the intended purchase, which can be matched against invoices received. This helps prevent discrepancies during GST audits and ensures smooth tax compliance.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about purchase orders in India

What is a Purchase Order?
A Purchase Order (PO) is a legally binding document issued by a buyer to a seller, detailing the types, quantities, and agreed prices for products or services. It serves as an official confirmation of a purchase and outlines the terms of the transaction.
Is a purchase order legally binding?
Yes, a purchase order becomes legally binding once accepted by the seller. It serves as a contract between buyer and seller, outlining the terms of the purchase including items, quantities, prices, delivery dates, and payment terms.
What is the difference between PO and invoice?
A Purchase Order (PO) is issued by the buyer before the purchase to request goods/services, while an invoice is issued by the seller after delivery to request payment. PO comes first in the transaction cycle, followed by the invoice.
What details should a purchase order contain?
A purchase order should contain: PO number and date, buyer and seller details (name, address, GST number), item descriptions, quantities, unit prices, tax rates, total amount, delivery date, payment terms, and authorized signatures.
Can I create a purchase order without GST number?
Yes, you can create a purchase order without a GST number if your business is not GST registered or if the transaction value is below the GST threshold. However, for B2B transactions in India, GST numbers are recommended for tax compliance.
How to generate a purchase order number?
Purchase order numbers are typically generated using a sequential format like PO-001, PO-002, or with date prefixes like PO-2026-001. You can also use custom formats like PO-COMPANY-DEPT-001. The key is to maintain a consistent, unique numbering system.
Can I edit a purchase order after sending it?
Yes, you can edit a purchase order after sending it, but you need to issue a revised PO with a new PO number or amendment reference. Both buyer and seller must agree to the changes, and the revised PO should clearly indicate it supersedes the previous one.
What is the validity period of a purchase order?
The validity period of a purchase order varies by company policy, typically ranging from 30 to 90 days. Some POs may have specific expiry dates mentioned. If not accepted within the validity period, the PO may lapse and require reissuance.
Can I cancel a purchase order?
Yes, you can cancel a purchase order, but the process depends on whether the seller has accepted it or started fulfilling the order. If not yet accepted, cancellation is straightforward. If work has begun, you may need to negotiate cancellation terms and potentially pay for work completed.
