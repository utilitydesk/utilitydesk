Understanding Income Tax for FY 2026-27

Financial Year 2026-27 (April 1, 2026 to March 31, 2027) offers taxpayers the choice between two tax regimes: the Old Regime with deductions and the New Regime with lower tax rates. Understanding both regimes is crucial for optimal tax planning.

Old Tax Regime vs New Tax Regime

The Old Tax Regime allows various deductions under sections like 80C, 80D, 24(b), and HRA exemption, but has higher tax rates. The New Tax Regime offers lower tax rates but eliminates most deductions, making it simpler but potentially more expensive for those with significant investments.

Key Deductions Under Old Regime

  • Section 80C: Up to ₹1.5 lakh for PF, ELSS, life insurance, tuition fees, and more
  • Section 80CCD(1B): Additional ₹50,000 for NPS contributions
  • Section 80D: Health insurance premiums up to ₹25,000 (₹50,000 for senior citizens)
  • Section 24(b): Home loan interest up to ₹2 lakh for self-occupied property
  • HRA Exemption: Based on actual rent paid and city of residence
  • Standard Deduction: ₹50,000 for salaried individuals (available in both regimes)

Which Regime Should You Choose?

The choice depends on your total deductions. As a thumb rule, if your deductions exceed ₹2-3 lakh, the Old Regime is likely more beneficial. Otherwise, the New Regime with its lower rates may save you more tax. Use this calculator to compare both regimes with your actual numbers and make an informed decision.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is FY 2026-27 and AY 2027-28?
FY 2026-27 (Financial Year) runs from April 1, 2026 to March 31, 2027. AY 2027-28 (Assessment Year) is the year following the financial year when your income is assessed and tax is calculated. Income earned in FY 2026-27 is taxed in AY 2027-28.
What are the new tax regime slabs for FY 2026-27?
New Tax Regime slabs for FY 2026-27: Nil up to ₹3 lakh, 5% for ₹3-6 lakh, 10% for ₹6-9 lakh, 15% for ₹9-12 lakh, 20% for ₹12-15 lakh, and 30% above ₹15 lakh. Standard deduction of ₹50,000 is available.
What are the old tax regime slabs for FY 2026-27?
Old Tax Regime slabs for individuals below 60 years: Nil up to ₹2.5 lakh, 5% for ₹2.5-5 lakh, 20% for ₹5-10 lakh, and 30% above ₹10 lakh. Senior citizens (60-80 years) get exemption up to ₹3 lakh, and super seniors (above 80) up to ₹5 lakh.
Which tax regime is better for me?
The better regime depends on your deductions. If your total deductions (80C, 80D, HRA, home loan interest, etc.) exceed ₹2-3 lakh, the old regime is likely better. Otherwise, the new regime with lower rates may save you more tax. Use this calculator to compare both regimes with your actual numbers.
What is the standard deduction for FY 2026-27?
For FY 2026-27, the standard deduction is ₹50,000 for salaried employees under both old and new tax regimes. This is available automatically without any proof or investment.
What deductions are available under the old regime?
Old regime allows deductions under: Section 80C (₹1.5 lakh for PF, ELSS, insurance), Section 80CCD(1B) (₹50,000 for NPS), Section 80D (₹25,000-₹50,000 for health insurance), Section 24(b) (₹2 lakh for home loan interest), HRA exemption, LTA, and professional tax.
Can I switch between old and new regime every year?
Yes, salaried individuals can switch between old and new tax regimes every year. However, business owners can only switch once. It's advisable to calculate your tax under both regimes each year and choose the one that results in lower tax liability.
What is health and education cess?
Health and education cess is 4% of the total income tax calculated. It's levied on the tax amount (not on income) and is used to fund health and education initiatives. This calculator automatically includes the 4% cess in the final tax calculation.
How is HRA exemption calculated?
HRA exemption is the least of: (1) Actual HRA received, (2) Rent paid minus 10% of basic salary, (3) 50% of basic salary for metro cities (Delhi, Mumbai, Kolkata, Chennai) or 40% for other cities. Use our HRA Calculator for precise calculation.
What is the surcharge on income tax?
Surcharge is an additional tax on income tax for high-income earners: 10% for income ₹50 lakh-1 crore, 15% for ₹1-2 crore, 25% for ₹2-5 crore, and 37% for above ₹5 crore. This calculator applies surcharge automatically based on your income level.
